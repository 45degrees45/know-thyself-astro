Every week has one non-negotiable output — something that exists in the world by Friday that did not exist on Monday. Jupiter retrograde will always find a reason to go back inward and refine. The plan names the output before the week begins so the retrograde pull has less room to operate.

Phase 1 · July 2026

Crystallise

The work of phase 1 is not doing — it is deciding. Who, what, and how. The outputs of these four weeks become the foundation that everything else is built on. Do not rush past them. Do not linger past them.

Week
1
1 – 7 July 2026
Who Exactly Am I Serving?
The most important question of the 90 days. Not what you know — who needs it and when.
The Three Tasks
  • Write 5 "pain situations" — specific moments when someone is in real need of the guidance you are most equipped to give. Make them scenes, not abstractions. ("When someone has just left a 15-year career and can't decide what to build next..." not "people in transition.")
  • For each scene: who is this person? Age, context, what they've already tried, what's at stake for them. Write it in second person — "you are 42, you have spent two decades..."
  • Pick ONE situation that feels most true to your depth AND most clear to you. Not the most noble one. The one you could navigate most confidently right now.
Daily Practice This Week
10-minute morning write
"The person I am most meant to help right now is ___." Start fresh each morning. Do not re-read previous days until Friday. On Friday, read all 7 and notice what stayed consistent.
Coaching Question (Sunday Evening)
For your journal
"If money and recognition were equal across all five situations, which would I most want to spend the next 6 months working inside? What does that tell me?"
Non-Negotiable Deliverable by Friday 7 July
One paragraph, written down: the person you serve and the specific situation you help them navigate. Concrete enough that someone who reads it could identify whether they're that person.
Week
2
8 – 14 July 2026
What Transformation Do I Create?
From "I help people with X" to "I take people FROM ___ TO ___."
The Three Tasks
  • For your chosen person-situation: write the "before" state in full detail — what they feel, think, fear, avoid, repeat. Make it uncomfortably specific. This is the sign that you actually understand the experience, not just the category.
  • Write the "after" state: what can they now see, decide, feel, or do that they could not before? What decision becomes possible? What weight lifts? What becomes clear?
  • Write the bridge: what is the essential move — the one thing that makes the transition happen? This is the seed of your method, your framework, your way of working. One or two sentences.
Daily Practice This Week
Re-reading ritual
Re-read your Week 1 deliverable each morning. Then add one sentence about the "before" experience of the person you serve. By Friday you'll have 7 lines that together describe the interior of the problem better than a day of planning could.
Coaching Question (Sunday Evening)
For your journal
"Have I personally been in the 'before' state I'm describing? What did it cost me — time, clarity, confidence, money, relationships? What do I know now that I wish someone had helped me see then?"
Non-Negotiable Deliverable by Friday 14 July
A written Before → After → Bridge statement. Three sentences maximum. If you cannot write it in three sentences, you haven't decided yet. Decide.
Week
3
15 – 21 July 2026
How Do I Offer This?
Design the first container — not the perfect offer, the first real one.
The Three Tasks
  • Design a 60–90 minute "depth conversation": what would you cover, in what sequence? What 3–5 questions would you ask? What insight do you aim to deliver before the end? Write it as a loose structure, not a rigid script.
  • Write the three things you want someone to walk away knowing or feeling: one thing about themselves, one thing about their situation, one thing about what becomes possible next. These are the outcome promises of your offer.
  • Give the conversation a working name. Not a final marketing name — just what you'd call it if describing it to a trusted friend. "A roots conversation." "A decision session." "A clarity audit." Whatever is true to how you think about it.
Daily Practice This Week
Out loud, every morning
Say your Before → After → Bridge statement aloud. 30 seconds. It will feel awkward and performative. Do it anyway. The awkwardness is Jupiter retrograde uncomfortable with external expression. The practice is training the expression muscle, not curing the feeling.
Coaching Question (Sunday Evening)
For your journal
"If I had to offer this conversation to one specific person I already know — starting next week — who would it be? What would I say to invite them? Write the actual words of the invitation."
Non-Negotiable Deliverable by Friday 21 July
A one-page description of your first offering: who it's for, what happens in it, what they walk away with, and the working name. This does not need to be polished. It needs to exist on paper.
Week
4
22 – 28 July 2026
First Real Conversations
Reaching out — not selling. Listening to how they describe the problem in their own words.
The Three Tasks
  • Identify 5 people in your existing network who are — right now — experiencing the "before" state you described. Do not filter for likelihood of becoming clients. Just identify the ones in the situation. Write their names and one sentence about why they fit.
  • Reach out to 3 of them this week. A genuine check-in with one specific question connected to their situation. Not: "I'm building something, want to help?" But: "I've been thinking about people who are navigating [their situation] — how is that going for you?" Listen more than you speak.
  • After each conversation: write what you learned. Focus specifically on the language they used — the words they reached for, the metaphors they chose, the things they said twice.
Daily Practice This Week
After every conversation
Write one sentence: "What I heard them say that I couldn't have said without them." This is the most valuable research you can do — gathering the language of the problem from the inside. Your marketing will eventually be built from these sentences, not from your own description of what you do.
Coaching Question (Sunday Evening)
For your journal
"During which conversation did I feel most alive and engaged — when was I leaning forward, when did time disappear? What was being discussed in that exact moment?"
Non-Negotiable Deliverable by Friday 28 July
3 conversations completed, notes from each written. Plus: one piece of their language you are going to borrow for your positioning — a phrase, a metaphor, a way of naming the problem that came from them, not you.

Activate

First Pilot Offer
Turning the conversation design into a real invitation to a real person.
The Three Tasks
  • From the 3 people you spoke to last week, identify the 1–2 who would benefit most from a depth session with you right now — based on where they are in the situation, not on who seems most enthusiastic or easiest to approach.
  • Write the actual invitation before you send it. One paragraph. Name what you observed in the conversation. Name what you'd explore in the session. Name what you're asking (time, and whether this is free/reciprocal/paid). Then send it without editing it for 3 more days.
  • Make the ask. One clear offer to one specific person this week. The Jupiter retrograde pattern generates endless reasons to refine before sending. Send it.

Morning write
"The value I could create for someone today is ___." Not "want to create" or "hope to create." Could. Present tense, confident. This is training Manipura — the solar plexus confidence that Saturn exalted H11 can fuel when it's consciously activated.

For your journal
"What felt most uncomfortable about making the offer? Where in my body did I feel it? What thought came immediately before I almost didn't send it?"
Non-Negotiable Deliverable by Friday 4 August
First pilot session booked — date confirmed. If it did not happen: send two more invitations before Sunday. The session must be confirmed, not merely considered.

Deliver the First Session
The most important week of the 90 days. Where depth becomes deployed wisdom.
The Three Tasks
  • Prepare — but not over-prepare. Spend 30 minutes the day before: what is the most important insight I want to offer this person? What is the one question that could shift how they see their situation? Write those down. Then set the preparation aside.
  • Deliver. Let the conversation be alive. Trust the depth — it has been there for decades. Your job is not to execute a perfect framework. It is to be fully present to this specific person and what they actually need.
  • After the session: write a full page, same day. What worked. What surprised you. What you wish you'd said. What they said that you want to remember. What you now know about your method that you didn't know going in.
Daily Practice This Week
Identity statement — every morning
"I am someone whose depth of understanding changes the trajectory of people's lives." Say it aloud. It will feel like an overclaim. It is not. Jupiter in own sign H1 with Hamsa Yoga is the astrological statement of exactly this. The resistance is the retrograde, not the truth.
Coaching Question (Sunday Evening)
For your journal
"At what point in the session did I feel most like myself — most at home in the conversation, most certain of what I was doing? What was I saying or asking in that exact moment? That is the core of my method."
Non-Negotiable Deliverable by Friday 11 August
First session delivered. Reflection page written and saved. And — ask the person one question after the session: "How would you describe what happened in our conversation to someone who asked you about it?" Write down their exact words.

Niche Lock
Deciding, not discovering. The niche is not found — it is chosen and committed to.
The Three Tasks
  • Based on 3 sessions and all conversations, write your final positioning statement: "I help [specific person] who is experiencing [specific situation] go from [before state] to [after state] through [your method]." One sentence. No qualifications, no "sometimes" or "depending on the situation."

  • Commit to it. You can refine the language later. The underlying choice — this person, this situation, this transformation — is made. Chosen niches feel constraining and freeing at the same time. That dual feeling is normal and correct.

Supporting Systems
The Daily System

Same structure, every day, regardless of which week you're in. Simple enough to do on a difficult day. Powerful enough to compound over 90 days.

Morning — 15 minutes
Ground + Set
4 counts in, 6 counts out — 5 breaths. Write the week's morning prompt (1 sentence). Write today's one action — specific, completable, output-focused. Say your positioning statement aloud. Begin.
Midday — 3 minutes
Reset
Stand up. Three long exhales. One question: "What have I put into the world today?" If the answer is nothing yet — what is the smallest useful thing I can do in the next 10 minutes? Do that thing first.
Evening — 10 minutes
Close + Forward
Write three things: what moved today (one observation), what felt uncomfortable (one honest note), and tomorrow's one action (written on paper, not a screen). Then close the work. The H12 Moon needs genuine rest — not continued processing under the name of preparation.
Weekly Review Format — Every Sunday

Spend 20–30 minutes on Sunday answering these four questions in writing. This is not optional — it is the feedback loop that makes the system self-correcting rather than self-defeating.

Question 1 — The Output
What exists now that did not exist on Monday? (Be specific — not "I made progress" but "I delivered one session, wrote one paragraph, sent two invitations.")
Question 2 — The Signal
What surprised me this week — about the people I spoke to, about my own response, about what landed and what didn't?
Question 3 — The Pattern
What do I now know about my work, my niche, or myself that I didn't know seven days ago? How does it change next week?
Question 4 — The Next Move
What is the single most important thing to do in the coming week? Not a list — one thing. Write it down, and start the week by doing it first.
When You're Stuck

The four states that break the momentum, and the one move that restores it in each.

Infinite Refinement Loop
You are back in Jupiter retrograde — reading one more book, refining the offer one more time, delaying the send because it isn't quite right yet. The move: Set a 25-minute timer. Before it ends, the thing must be sent, posted, or delivered. Not perfect. Sent.
Interior Withdrawal
H12 Moon has pulled the energy inward. Everything feels too exposed or too raw. The move: Do not fight it. Take 24 hours of genuine rest — not planning, not researching, not "getting ready." Then return to the smallest possible output: one sentence, one message, one question asked. The momentum restores from small, not from force.
Meaning Fatigue
You have been processing without transmitting and the well feels heavy. The move: Have one genuine conversation — not a pitch, not a coaching session, just a real exchange with someone about something you care about. The well lightens when water moves through it.
Comparison Collapse
You've seen someone else doing something similar and the retrograde Jupiter immediately demotes your own worth. The move: Open your journal and read the reflection from your best session. You are not competing with them. You are serving the specific people who need exactly the depth you have — which is different from what anyone else has.
