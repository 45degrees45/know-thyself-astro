Animals of the Chart

Sagittarius 6.5° · 3 March 2002 · Alleppey, Kerala
In Vedic astrology, each of the 27 nakshatras carries a yoni — a sacred animal energy. Every planet placed in a nakshatra takes on that animal's qualities. Together, these animals form a living portrait far richer than any single symbol. Ruhi's chart holds seven distinct animal energies, with a striking double convergence: the Horse appears three times, and the Dog appears twice — giving this chart two unmistakable primary signatures.
🐎
Primary Symbol
The Horse
Ashva · The Divine Twin Horses of Ashwini
Mars exalted Ashwini H5 · Sun in Shatabhisha H3 · Sagittarius Centaur Lagna
Three independent sources in Ruhi's chart all point to the same animal. Mars, exalted at 7.0° Aries, sits in Ashwini nakshatra — the nakshatra symbolised by the horse's head, named for the divine physician twins (Ashwini Kumaras) who ride horses at the speed of dawn. The Sun sits in Shatabhisha, whose yoni is the female horse — the light-bearer, the healer's companion. And Sagittarius itself is the centaur — the half-human, half-horse archer who shoots toward truth from a gallop. The Horse is not metaphor here. It is the literal architecture: a chart built for speed, forward motion, healing at full charge, and a soul that was never meant to stand still.
The Lagna Animal — The Essential Soul
🐕
The Dog
Shvana · Nirriti's Hound — The Root-Seeker
Sagittarius Lagna 6.5° Mula Nakshatra · Ketu-ruled
The soul beneath all other animals
Sagittarius at 6.5° places the Lagna in Mula nakshatra — the nakshatra of roots, of dissolution, of going to the very origin of things. Mula means "root." Its deity is Nirriti, the goddess of destruction and un-making. Its animal is the male dog: not the domestic companion, but the dark hound who follows a scent into the underworld without hesitation. Mula's dog cannot rest in the shallows. It digs. It must find the source — of the pain, of the truth, of the deepest why. Sagittarius wants to range philosophically and freely, but Mula's dog keeps pulling the archer back to ground, insisting on roots before distances. The remarkable fact: Jupiter (Lagna lord) also carries dog energy in Ardra nakshatra — meaning Ruhi's soul-nature and her wisdom-bearer are both dogs. This chart has an unshakeable loyalty to truth running all the way down.
Goes to the root Loyal to truth above comfort Follows the scent underground Cannot rest in the superficial Soul of the seeker

Sun in Shatabhisha H3 · Rahu-ruled
The healer's light — the medicine-bearing mare
The Sun sits in Shatabhisha nakshatra — the hundred healers, ruled by Rahu, governed by Varuna (the deity of cosmic law and healing waters). Its yoni is the female horse: the mare who carries the medicine lamp. Where Mars (male horse, Ashwini) is the warrior-charger, this Sun-horse is the healer. Together they form the Ashwini Kumara pair — the divine physician twins who gallop at dawn, restoring what the night broke. Sun in H3 (courage, communication, creative initiative) means this solar horse expresses through bold self-expression and courageous speech. The healer who names the wound.

The Buffalo
Mahisha · Male Buffalo
Moon in Swati H11 · Rahu-ruled
The wind-buffalo — carries weight alone
The Moon sits in Swati nakshatra — the nakshatra of the wind god Vayu, whose symbol is a young sapling bending without breaking. Swati's yoni is the male buffalo: the great beast who walks into the gale without flinching. Kemadruma Yoga (Moon flanked by empty houses) means this buffalo walks largely alone through the social and emotional world. But alone does not mean weak. The buffalo carries extraordinary weight across open terrain that others cannot endure. Moon in H11 (gains, aspirations, social belonging) — this wind-buffalo crosses the social plain with endurance, bending through storms of belonging and isolation, never breaking.

The Stallion
Ashva · Male Horse · Exalted
Mars exalted Ashwini H5 · Peak Power
The warrior-healer — the chart's most powerful force
Mars exalted at 7.0° Aries in Ashwini — the male horse at absolute peak. Ashwini is Mars's deepest natural home: the nakshatra named for horse-headed divine twins who travel at the speed of light and heal whatever they touch. The exalted stallion in H5 (creativity, passion, intelligence, children, risk) gallops through creative terrain at full charge. This horse does not tire. It does not stop for pain — it runs through it. This is the engine of the entire chart. Every other animal draws energy from this Mars-Horse's fire. The healer who charges forward first and asks questions after.

The Monkey
Markata · Male Monkey
Mercury in Shravana H2 · Moon-ruled
The listening intelligence — hears before it speaks
Mercury sits in Shravana nakshatra — the listener, the nakshatra of Vishnu's three steps, ruled by the Moon. Shravana's deepest teaching: hear before you say. Its yoni is the male monkey: quick, adaptive, alert, swinging between ideas with fluid intelligence — but grounded here in Vishnu's ordered wisdom. This is not the mischievous monkey. It is the attentive student who collects everything first and speaks last. Mercury in H2 (speech, accumulated knowledge, family, the face) places this listening monkey in the house of voice itself. Ruhi absorbs far more than she reveals — and when she speaks, the precision surprises people.
Listens before speaking Absorbs everything Surprising precision Adaptive intelligence

The Storm-Dog
Shvana · Female Dog
Jupiter in Ardra H7 · Rahu-ruled · Lagna Lord
Wisdom through storms — loyal to what matters
Jupiter — Ruhi's Lagna lord, the planet that holds her soul — sits in Ardra nakshatra, whose deity is Rudra (the storm god) and whose animal is the female dog: Shiva's faithful companion who follows him into the cremation ground without flinching. Ardra's symbol is the teardrop — the storm that breaks something open and makes it real. Jupiter in H7 means Ruhi's deepest wisdom emerges through relationship, and specifically through relationships that go through storms. The dog teaches that love is not shown in good weather — it is shown in the willingness to stay when the sky breaks. Both Lagna and Lagna lord carry dog energy: loyalty to truth is not a style. It is the soul.
Stays through the storm Wisdom forged in grief Loyal past reason Follows into darkness

The Lion
Simha · Male Lion
Venus in Purva Bhadrapada H3 · Jupiter-ruled
The philosophical fire-lion — charismatic in its domain
Venus sits in Purva Bhadrapada nakshatra — the former lucky feet, ruled by Jupiter, with the deity Aja Ekapada (the one-footed fire-serpent). Its symbol is the sword. Its animal is the male lion: not the crowd-performer, but the solitary, philosophical lion who has looked into the abyss and roared anyway. Purva Bhadrapada is the nakshatra of transformation through fire and extreme devotion. Venus in H3 (creative self-expression, courage, communication) places this lion-energy in the house of voice and creative output. When Ruhi speaks in her element — about what she knows and loves — she does so with the lion's natural authority. People listen. She may not notice how much.
Natural authority Philosophical fire Charismatic in domain Transformed by intensity

The Serpent
Sarpa · Male Serpent
Saturn in Rohini H6 · Moon-ruled
Ancient patience — coiled in the house of obstacles
Saturn sits in Rohini nakshatra — the most fertile, abundant, and creatively rich nakshatra in the zodiac (ruled by the Moon, starring Aldebaran). Rohini's yoni is the male serpent: the cobra who holds still, appears dormant, and carries lifetimes of distilled knowledge in its stillness. Saturn (the planet of discipline and restriction) in Rohini channels this abundance through the lens of earned endurance. In H6 (obstacles, health, service, enemies), the serpent is the guardian of the threshold — patient, accumulating power quietly, striking with absolute precision only when the moment is exact. Every obstacle Ruhi faces wraps the serpent another coil tighter. The venom is wisdom, collected slowly over years.
Ancient patience Strikes precisely Wisdom as venom Obstacles as teachers

The Searching Serpent
Sarpa · Female Serpent
Rahu in Mrigashira H6 · Mars-ruled · Serpent Pair
The seeker who follows the deer into the dark forest
Rahu sits in Mrigashira nakshatra — the deer's head, the nakshatra of Soma (the Moon) and the eternal search. Mrigashira's symbol is the deer's head: always sniffing the wind, drawn forward by a scent just out of reach. Its yoni is the female serpent: the searching one who follows the deer's trail through darkness. Both Saturn and Rahu sit in H6 — two serpents together in the same house. This is the rarest pairing in Ruhi's chart. The male serpent (Saturn) holds still in patient guardianship. The female serpent (Rahu) perpetually searches — hunting for the solution, the remedy, the way through the obstacle. Together they make the sixth house a field of profound, serpentine power.
Follows the unseen scent Perpetual seeker Hunts through darkness Serpent-pair power in H6

The Deer
Mriga · Male Deer
Ketu in Jyeshtha H12 · Mercury-ruled · Past Life
The eldest — dignity dissolving into liberation
Ketu sits in Jyeshtha nakshatra — the eldest, the nakshatra of Indra (king of the gods), its symbol the circular amulet or the royal umbrella. Its yoni is the male deer: the stag who carries the amulet of authority. Ketu dissolves what it touches. In H12 (liberation, hidden realms, moksha, loss), Ketu releases the past-life memories stored in Jyeshtha. The deer of previous lives held position, sovereignty, leadership — was the eldest, the chief, the one others deferred to. That authority now feeds into the spiritual reservoir rather than the material world. Ruhi carries ancestral dignity she may sense as a vague feeling of having once known more, led more, been more. The deer remembers. Ketu lets go.
Ancestral authority Past-life leadership Releases what is held Dignity dissolving to grace
